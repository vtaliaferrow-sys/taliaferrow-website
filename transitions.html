Transitions

Real estate representation for consequential moments of change.

Life transitions require more than marketing and negotiation. They require structure, coordination, and the ability to guide people through consequential change with clarity and care.

Find Your Situation
Estate & Probate Divorce Downsizing Lifestyle Changes

Real estate is often the visible component of a much larger transition already underway: the settlement of an estate, a divorce, a parent moving into care, a generational move, a financial restructuring, or simply the recognition that a home no longer fits the next stage of life.

In these moments, the property is rarely the only thing happening and almost never the most difficult part.

The role of The Taliaferrow Team is to bring steadiness, organization, and experienced guidance to the real estate component while clients and families navigate everything surrounding it.

How We Work

A different kind of representation.

Traditional real estate transactions are often built around speed, visibility, and momentum.

Transitions require something different.

The Work Requires

  • Thoughtful pacing
  • Clear communication
  • Coordination between professionals
  • Emotional discretion
  • Strategic timing
  • The ability to reduce friction during already difficult circumstances

Those Involved Often Include

  • Adult children and multiple decision-makers
  • Care managers and transition specialists
  • Estate and probate attorneys
  • Matrimonial counsel
  • Fiduciaries and trustees
  • Financial advisors and CPAs
The Standard The process must remain organized, calm, and carefully managed from beginning to end.
Specific Situations

Every transition has its own shape.

— I

Estate & Probate

The sale of inherited property often arrives during periods of grief, legal coordination, and family complexity.

Viola works alongside executors, attorneys, and beneficiaries to help organize the sale process, prepare the property appropriately for the market, and coordinate the many moving parts that estate transactions frequently involve.

Some families require a highly visible marketing approach. Others require privacy and discretion. Both are handled thoughtfully and strategically.

— II

Divorce & Matrimonial Transitions

Real estate decisions during divorce proceedings require neutrality, discretion, and careful communication.

The Taliaferrow Team works with spouses, attorneys, and financial professionals to help navigate property sales where emotions, timing, and legal considerations are often deeply interconnected.

The objective is not simply to close a transaction, but to move the process forward as constructively and professionally as possible.

— III

Downsizing & Senior Transitions

Leaving a long-held home can be emotionally and logistically overwhelming.

As a Seniors Real Estate Specialist (SRES®), Viola understands the layers often involved:

  • Timing
  • Family coordination
  • Disposition of belongings
  • Future housing decisions
  • Financial considerations
  • The emotional weight of transition itself

The process is approached patiently, respectfully, and without pressure.

— IV

Generational & Lifestyle Changes

Not every transition begins with difficulty. Some begin with opportunity:

  • A growing family
  • A return to New York
  • A quieter lifestyle
  • Proximity to children or grandchildren
  • Architectural priorities
  • A change in pace
  • Or the simple desire for a different way of living

These transitions deserve the same level of thoughtful representation and strategic guidance.

Why It Works

Structure in complex situations.

Before entering real estate, Viola spent years managing enterprise telecommunications projects involving multiple stakeholders, immovable deadlines, and high-level coordination.

That background continues to shape the way the team approaches transitional real estate today:

  • Organized communication
  • Steady oversight
  • Coordination between professionals
  • Attention to detail
  • Calm execution under pressure

Because in consequential moments, clarity matters.

Begin a Conversation

There is no pressure to decide anything.

Many engagements begin with a quiet call from a family member, fiduciary, or attorney simply seeking perspective on the situation. Viola listens first, considers the circumstances carefully, and offers an honest assessment of the available options and whether moving forward makes sense at all. The first conversation is private, thoughtful, and without obligation.
